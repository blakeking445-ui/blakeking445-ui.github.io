Reverse Engineered Fuel Door Mount

Developed a structured workflow to analyze dimensional deviation between a machined component and its CAD model using heat map visualization. The process involved aligning scan data to the original CAD geometry and computing point-to-surface deviations to quantify manufacturing errors across the entire part. These deviations were visualized as a color-mapped heat map, allowing quick identification of localized inaccuracies such as overcuts, undercuts, and surface distortion. This method improved inspection efficiency by replacing manual measurements with a full-field comparison approach, enabling clearer and more consistent validation of machining quality against design specifications.
